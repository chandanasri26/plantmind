1. SAFETY PROTOCOLS FOR HIGH-PRESSURE STEAM BOILERS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
High-pressure steam boilers (operating above 15 psi / 1 bar) represent extreme 
thermal and physical hazards. The following safety protocols are mandatory for all 
operating technicians:

* DAILY SAFETY BLOWDOWN: Technicians must perform blowdowns on the water columns 
  and gauge glasses daily to clear sludge and verify water level float switches.
* ACCUMULATION TESTS: Run an accumulation test annually. The safety valve must 
  be capable of discharging all steam generated by the boiler without allowing 
  pressure to rise more than 6% above the maximum allowable working pressure (MAWP).
* MINIMUM TEMPERATURE LIMITS: Never fire a cold boiler rapidly. Warm up the 
  refractory and metal components gradually, maintaining a rate of temperature 
  rise not exceeding 100°F (38°C) per hour to prevent thermal shock fractures.
* EMERGENCY SHUTDOWN TRIGGERS: Immediately cut fuel supply and isolate steam lines 
  if:
  a. Low Water Cutoff (LWCO) fails to trigger during low-water conditions.
  b. Boiler pressure exceeds MAWP by more than 10% and safety valves fail to lift.
  c. Flame failure (flameout) occurs without automated purge sequencing.


2. BOILER FEED PUMP (BFP-02) VIBRATION TROUBLESHOOTING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Equipment ID: BFP-02
Type: Multi-stage Centrifugal Feedwater Pump
Driver: 450 HP Induction Motor, 3600 RPM

Vibration anomalies on BFP-02 must be diagnosed using the following decision tree:

* HIGH RADIAL VIBRATION AT 1X RUNNING SPEED (3600 CPM):
  - Primary Cause: Mechanical Unbalance or Bent Shaft.
  - Action: Inspect impeller for cavitation pitting or scale build-up. Run dial 
    indicator check on shaft runout. Rebalance rotor dynamically.

* HIGH RADIAL VIBRATION AT 2X RUNNING SPEED (7200 CPM):
  - Primary Cause: Mechanical Misalignment.
  - Action: Perform laser alignment checks on the pump-to-motor coupling. 
    Verify soft foot conditions using a micrometer. Adjust shim packs at driver base.

* HIGH AXIAL VIBRATION WITH PHASE DIFFERENCE:
  - Primary Cause: Misalignment or coupling damage.
  - Action: Replace worn coupling insert (elastomeric or grid). Check thrust 
    bearing clearances.

* HIGH FREQUENCY BROADBAND NOISE/VIBRATION (20 kHz to 50 kHz):
  - Primary Cause: Cavitation due to low Net Positive Suction Head Available (NPSHa).
  - Action: Verify deaerator level. Inspect suction strainers for blockage. 
    Partially throttle discharge valve if operating outside design envelope (too far right on curve).


3. LOCKOUT/TAGOUT (LOTO) PROCEDURES FOR UTILITIES DEPT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
LOTO is required before any worker performs maintenance where unexpected start-up 
or release of stored energy could cause injury.

* STEP 1: PREPARATION & NOTIFICATION
  - Identify all energy sources (electrical, hydraulic, pneumatic, steam pressure).
  - Notify affected operators in the boiler controls area.

* STEP 2: EQUIPMENT SHUTDOWN
  - De-energize the unit via the local control panel (stop sequence).

* STEP 3: ENERGY ISOLATION
  - Turn off and lock the main electrical circuit breaker (480V Sub-panel B-3, Breaker #12).
  - Chain and padlock the steam supply gate valve (V-301) and feedwater inlet valve (V-104).

* STEP 4: APPLY LOTO DEVICES & TAGS
  - Apply personal padlocks and tags to each isolation point.
  - Tags must state: "DANGER - DO NOT OPERATE - MAINTENANCE IN PROGRESS", signed 
    by the lead technician with contact details.

* STEP 5: STORED ENERGY RELEASE
  - Open drain valves to relieve residual hydraulic or steam pressure.
  - Discharge capacitive electrical loads if applicable.

* STEP 6: VERIFICATION OF ISOLATION (ZERO ENERGY STATE)
  - Crucial Step: Attempt to restart the equipment locally to confirm isolation. 
    Ensure all technicians are clear before testing. Return switch to "OFF" position.


4. LEVEL TRANSMITTER (LT-101) CALIBRATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Location: Deaerator Storage Tank D-101
Type: Guided Wave Radar (GWR) level transmitter

* CALIBRATION FREQUENCY: Guided wave radar transmitters must be calibrated every 
  12 months, or immediately following any steam pressure transient exceeding 300 psi.
* ZERO POINT REFERENCE: 0% level corresponds to 12.5 inches (318 mm) above the 
  bottom of the deaerator tank shell.
* FIELD CALIBRATION STEPS:
  1. Establish local bypass loop to prevent boiler feedwater starvation trip.
  2. Connect field communicator (HART) to the loop terminals (24V DC loop).
  3. Drain level chamber completely. Verify transmitter output reads 4.00 mA (±0.02 mA).
  4. Fill chamber slowly to the 100% tap. Verify transmitter output reads 20.00 mA (±0.02 mA).
  5. If trim is required, adjust the Zero/Span values via the HART communicator.
